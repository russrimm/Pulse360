## Outcome
**Verdict: 🔴 CRITICAL** on current state.

Key findings:
1. 8 of 9 `dangerouslySetInnerHTML` sites render untrusted upstream HTML with zero sanitization
2. `MessageCard.tsx` uses DOMPurify only for text-preview (truncated string), NOT for full content render
3. Active XSS / phishing / misattribution surface on public-facing mspulse360.app

Mandated remediation:
- Centralized `sanitizeFeedHtml()` at server-side ingestion (`api.server.ts`)
- DOMPurify with explicit ALLOWED_TAGS / ALLOWED_ATTR curated config
- `https?`-only protocol allowlist (block `javascript:`, `data:`, `vbscript:`)
- Link hardening: `rel="noopener noreferrer"`, `target="_blank"` on all anchor tags
- `MessageDetail` must sanitize AFTER its regex h2-transform (not before, or transform re-introduces risk)

Residual risks requiring human decision:
1. Inline `style` attribute allowlist (broad risk vs. rendering fidelity)
2. External feed `<img>` — CSP policy and/or image proxy
3. Feed-endpoint misattribution trust model (how much to trust Microsoft-sourced feeds)

Sanitization rollout elevated to **step 1 / top priority** in Alex's roadmap as a result.
